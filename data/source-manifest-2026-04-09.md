# Source Manifest

## Primary benchmark

### FLAb

- Source: GitHub repository `Graylab/FLAb`
- URL: https://github.com/Graylab/FLAb
- Why we use it: public therapeutic antibody benchmark with multiple developability-related assay categories
- Intended role in project: primary supervised benchmark

## Supporting literature benchmark

### MINT paper

- Source: Nature Communications
- URL: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-025-67971-3
- Why we use it: confirms that FLAb is already used in recent antibody representation benchmarking and provides a reproducible benchmark framing
- Intended role in project: benchmark justification and model-comparison framing

## Sequence support database

### PLAbDab

- Source: Oxford Protein Informatics Group
- URL: https://opig.stats.ox.ac.uk/webapps/plabdab/
- Why we use it: large paired antibody sequence resource with literature links
- Intended role in project: exploratory antigen-focused retrieval and metadata support

## Structural support database

### SAbDab

- Source: Oxford Protein Informatics Group
- URL: https://opig.stats.ox.ac.uk/webapps/sabdab-sabpred/sabdab
- Why we use it: structural antibody database with standardized annotations
- Intended role in project: structural context and optional case-study support

## Therapeutic support database

### Thera-SAbDab

- Source: Oxford Protein Informatics Group / Nucleic Acids Research
- URL: https://academic.oup.com/nar/article/48/D1/D383/5573951
- Why we use it: therapeutic antibody metadata and structural linkage
- Intended role in project: therapeutic target context, not primary labels

## Design motivation paper

### RFdiffusion antibody paper

- Source: Nature
- URL: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-09721-5
- Why we use it: motivates why antibody design is interesting
- Intended role in project: motivation only, not the core experimental template
